Macedonian Chicken Rice Pilaf

  • 1(4-lb.) chicken
  • 2 long green pepper
  • 4 celery stalks, cut in thirds
  • 1 celery root cut in thirds
  • 2 parsnips, cut in thirds
  • 1/2 c. chopped onions
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 T. chopped parsley
  • 2 c. Arborio rice, uncooked
  • 4 carrots, peeled and cut in thirds

Place 3 quarts of water in a pot. Wash and clean chicken inside and out. Place in the pot. Bring to a boil. Skim off the top. Cook for 1/2 hour. Add carrots, celery root, parsnip, celery stalk and seasonings to taste. Cover and simmer 2 hous. Remove chicken from soup and clean from skin and bones. Strain soup and reserve 6 cups for pilaf. To degrease soup, refrigerate for several hours or overnight and remove congealed fat from top of soup. Place chicken broth in a medium saucepan; bring to a simmer. Coat a medium frying pan with 2 tablespoons olive oil; place over medium heat until hot. Add onion and 1 italian long pepper, diced. Saute untiltender. Add rice and cook 3 minutes stirring constantly. Add 1/2 cup warm broth, cook 5 minutes or until liquid is nearly absorbed, stirring constantly. Add remaining broth, 1/2 cup at a time. Place in a baking dish, add chicken pieces and 1 long red or green pepper, 2 tablespoons of chopped parsley and mix well. Taste for salt, freshly ground pepper. Bake at 350 degrees for 30 minutes or until it's lightly browned on top.
